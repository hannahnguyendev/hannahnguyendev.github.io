Turf Wars Robot:"Fantastic"

Our team designed and built a compact robot with a gear-train forklift and claw combo, all fully 3D CAD-modeled in SolidWorks and created entirely from scratch using the lathe, CNC mill, 3D-printer, laser cutter, horizontal and vertical bandsaws, drill press, and a motor dissected from an electronic screwdriver.

Dance Dance Glow Grid

The Glow Grid is an interactive game that combines Dance Dance Revolution and Whack-a-Mole, designed to test players' reaction times while encouraging movement and fun. The project features a wooden frame containing hand-made pressure-sensitive dance tiles connected to LED strips.

I co-developed the Arduino code to control the eight LED strips and pressure sensors, implementing random tile activation, reaction time tracking, and game logic as well as configured pull-up resistors for the pressure sensors. I also helped create the frame and tiles.








Feed the Trees!

During Conflux Winter Residency, I worked with another fellow to create an immerisve experience featuring a parasitic tree! I led the hardware aspect while they led the VR experience (both of us created the physical tree out of recycled materials).

The tree "heart" is a capacitive touch device that uses heart sensors and vibration motors to simulate your blood going into the tree. During the exhibition of the art residency, we were able to talk to numerous people including a professor from MIT Media Lab and a curator of an art gallery in Sweden (whom Conflux is now working with to create a new exhibit)!






BlackBox Gallery

BlackBox Gallery is a project I co-founded for my club Conflux (Art Tech). We wanted to create a showcase room that could be altered however wanted by student artists. It ended up being a 16 x 12 x 10 ft room.

We built everything from scratch, connecting the walls with brackets so it could be disconnected and relocated in the future. There are beams at the top for projectors and we will be showcasing our first artist in spring!
