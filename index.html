Research Internship: Biomimetic Vertebral Columns (BVCs)!

For my spring semester as well as summer, I worked with a gradudate student in the Stephanie E Pierce Lab to replicate early tetrapod spinal morphology in 3Matic (a CAD software mainly used for bio/medical engineering purposes)! We also 3D designed molds for the silicone parts of the model and used a 3D printer to mass-produce.

We used the Lauder Flapper to test our BVCs in water under specific conditions to analyze how different centrum shapes affect the tradeoff between flexibility and stability. There are dozens of variations of centrum shapes and sizes we had to keep accounted and documented for testing!








Bridge Competition: Chappell Prone to Buckle

As part of the ES120 Bridge Building Competition, our team engineered a high-performance arch bridge constructed from plywood, spanning 12 inches with a 2 inch height, adhering to a 6:1 span-to-depth ratio to prevent buckling and minimize deflection. On the Instron, our bridge achieved a 1250:1 strength-to-weight ratio, supporting 250 kgf at only 0.2 kg and complied with strict spatial constraints!

We designed and 3D modeled the bridge in SolidWorks, then used finite element analysis (FEA) to simulate stress concentrations and deflection behavior under load. The structure utilized a triangulated truss system and branching members to distribute forces efficiently, with top bracing to reinforce regions under high compressive stress and larger base mounts at key tensile stress points. Our failure analysis aligned closely with simulation results, fractures occuring at the corners of the arch. All components were laser-cut and assembled using non-structural adhesives.








Turf Wars Robot:"Fantastic"

Our team designed and built a compact robot with a gear-train forklift and claw combo, all fully 3D CAD-modeled in SolidWorks and created entirely from scratch using the lathe, CNC mill, 3D-printer, laser cutter, horizontal and vertical bandsaws, drill press, and a motor dissected from an electronic screwdriver.

It’s built to grab, lift, handle tricky terrains, stack, and move objects efficiently. I modeled the claw arms, motor mount, and silicone molds for the grip in SolidWorks and used the mill, drill-press, lathe, laser-cutter, and 3D-printer to create everything. Our robot won 2nd place!








Dance Dance Glow Grid

The Glow Grid is an interactive game that combines Dance Dance Revolution and Whack-a-Mole, designed to test players' reaction times while encouraging movement and fun. The project features a wooden frame containing hand-made pressure-sensitive dance tiles connected to LED strips.

I co-developed the Arduino code to control the eight LED strips and pressure sensors, implementing random tile activation, reaction time tracking, and game logic as well as configured pull-up resistors for the pressure sensors. I also helped create the frame and tiles.








Feed the Trees!

During Conflux Winter Residency, I worked with another fellow to create an immerisve experience featuring a parasitic tree! I led the hardware aspect while they led the VR experience (both of us created the physical tree out of recycled materials).

The tree "heart" is a capacitive touch device that uses heart sensors and vibration motors to simulate your blood going into the tree. During the exhibition of the art residency, we were able to talk to numerous people including a professor from MIT Media Lab and a curator of an art gallery in Sweden (whom Conflux is now working with to create a new exhibit)!






BlackBox Gallery

BlackBox Gallery is a project I co-founded for my club Conflux (Art Tech). We wanted to create a showcase room that could be altered however wanted by student artists. It ended up being a 16 x 12 x 10 ft room.

We built everything from scratch, connecting the walls with brackets so it could be disconnected and relocated in the future. There are beams at the top for projectors and we will be showcasing our first artist in spring!
