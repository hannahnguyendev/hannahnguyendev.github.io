ES96: The Bionausaureses FUEL CELL

Biodegradable Gas Generation System

This project explores a sustainable alternative to traditional robotic power systems by developing a fully biodegradable gas generation mechanism. Instead of relying on batteries or electronics, the system uses a chemical reaction between yeast and hydrogen peroxide to produce oxygen gas for propulsion.

Designed for environmental monitoring applications, this system reduces long-term pollution from abandoned underwater robots. A controlled dispensing mechanism releases yeast capsules into hydrogen peroxide, enabling sustained gas generation and pressure buildup. The result is a lightweight, non-toxic, and environmentally friendly propulsion method for biodegradable robotic platforms.
